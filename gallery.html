Medical Clinic

A clinic website that welcomes every patient in their own language.

The problem

A large portion of the clinic's patients were non-native English speakers, many of whom weren't particularly comfortable with technology. The old site was a confusing wall of information that didn't serve either group — patients were calling reception for things the website should have answered.

The solution

We redesigned the flow from the ground up, with large tap-targets, plain language, and a structure that reads naturally for less tech-savvy users. On top of that, we built full custom translations across the entire site into four languages — not a plugin, not Google Translate auto-detection. Every word, properly localised.

The result

Reception calls for basic queries dropped noticeably, and non-English-speaking patients finally had an online experience that didn't feel like a barrier. Booking completions went up across all four language audiences.

Beauty & Lash Salon

A new site that slots straight into the booking system she already ran her business on.

The problem

Jade had built a strong local following and a booking system her clients already knew inside out — but her website didn't match the quality of her work, and rebuilding it risked disrupting a booking flow that was already doing the job. Any new site had to respect what was already working.

The solution

A fully custom, editorial-feel site built around the brand — with her existing booking system and policies integrated seamlessly, not replaced. Clients move from homepage to "Book now" without noticing a handoff; the hours, deposits, and cancellation policies live in their natural home. Zero re-learning for repeat clients, a much nicer first impression for new ones.

The result

A site that finally matched the calibre of the work — without disturbing the engine underneath. Returning clients kept booking the way they always had; new clients got a much more compelling reason to book in the first place.

SaaS Platform

Making a creator-focused SaaS feel authentic, not copy-pasted.

The problem

The product was aimed at online content creators — an audience that's seen every "grow your following 10x" scam on the internet. Generic stock visuals, recycled SaaS templates, and cookie-cutter copy would have killed trust on sight, even though the product was genuinely solid.

The solution

A fully custom build with every visual coded from scratch — the live crawler on the laptop, the animated phone conversation, the custom feature cards, the data visualisations. Nothing dragged-and-dropped, no stock mockups, no generic illustrations. Everything rendered in JavaScript and built to feel like a real tool made by real people.

The result

A site that stands out in a sea of SaaS sameness. Sign-up conversion went up, trust signals improved measurably, and the brand finally had visual credibility to match what the product actually delivers.

Metal Polishing

A specialist trade site that looks as premium as the work it sells.

The problem

Metal polishing is a niche, high-skill trade — but most competitor websites in the space looked like they were built in 2008. The business was losing higher-value commercial jobs to rivals who didn't do better work, just had a more credible online presence. First impressions were costing quotes.

The solution

A dark, confident, photo-forward site that treats the finished work as the hero — mirror-polish close-ups, before/afters, and a clear breakdown of what the business actually does. An integrated quote form captures project details upfront so the owner doesn't waste time on back-and-forth emails just to get basic information.

The result

Quote enquiries went up, and the quality of those enquiries improved — larger jobs, more commercial clients, fewer "how much for a bumper" time-wasters. The site now matches the calibre of the work.
